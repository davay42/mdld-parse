# Medical Imaging AI Research {=research:paper .prov:Entity label}

This paper explores how deep learning attention mechanisms can improve automated diagnosis from medical imaging data, covering the full arc from dataset assembly through model training to peer-reviewed publication.

## Authors {=research:authors .prov:Entity label}

The work was led by **[Dr. Sarah Martinez](mailto:sarah.martinez@mit.edu)** {rdfs:label} {research:email} at MIT, who served as [Lead Researcher] {research:role} and was responsible for [methodology development] {research:contribution}. Joining her was **[Dr. James Liu](mailto:james.liu@mit.edu)** {=research:james-liu .prov:Person rdfs:label research:email}, the team's [ML Engineer] {research:role}, who took charge of [model implementation] {research:contribution}.

## Research Activity {=research:research-activity .prov:Activity label}

The project ran from [2024-01-01] {prov:startedAtTime ^^xsd:date} through [2024-03-15] {prov:endedAtTime ^^xsd:date}, carried out by the [Research Team] {+research:authors ?prov:wasAssociatedWith} according to the [Medical AI Research Plan] {+research:plan .prov:Plan ?prov:hadPlan} they had drafted the previous autumn.

## Dataset Collection {=research:dataset-collection .prov:Activity label}

Assembling a dataset of sufficient quality and scale was the first major challenge.

### Primary Dataset {=data:primary-dataset .research:Dataset .prov:Entity label}

The team sourced [80,000] {data:trainCount ^^xsd:integer} DICOM training images from the [GitHub Medical Imaging Repository] {research:source}, setting aside [10,000] {data:valCount ^^xsd:integer} for validation and another [10,000] {data:testCount ^^xsd:integer} for final testing. The images span [15] {data:classCount ^^xsd:integer} anatomical structures. It was assembled during the [dataset collection phase] {+research:dataset-collection ?prov:wasGeneratedBy}.

### Augmented Dataset {=data:augmented-dataset .research:Dataset .prov:Entity label}

Working from that [primary collection] {+data:primary-dataset ?prov:wasDerivedFrom}, the team produced a [512x512] {data:resolution} grayscale augmented set enriched with [patient metadata] {data:features}, covering [1,000] {data:patientCount ^^xsd:integer} individual patients to reduce demographic bias. This derived set was also [generated] {+research:dataset-collection ?prov:wasGeneratedBy} as part of the same collection activity.

## Model Development {=research:model-development .prov:Activity label}

[Dr. James Liu] {+research:james-liu ?prov:wasAssociatedWith} drove the implementation, feeding the model both the [raw] {+data:primary-dataset ?prov:used} and [augmented] {+data:augmented-dataset ?prov:used} datasets. The process produced a [trained model artifact] {+research:trained-model ?prov:generated} ready for evaluation.

## Model Evaluation {=research:evaluation .prov:Activity label}

Evaluation began on [2024-03-01] {prov:startedAtTime ^^xsd:date}. The [trained model] {+research:trained-model ?prov:used} was put through a comprehensive test suite that yielded the [evaluation results] {+research:eval-results ?prov:generated} reported below.

### Performance Metrics {=research:metrics .prov:Entity label}

The results were strong across the board. Training accuracy reached [94.5%] {research:trainAcc ^^xsd:decimal}, with validation holding at [92.3%] {research:valAcc ^^xsd:decimal} and the held-out test set at [91.8%] {research:testAcc ^^xsd:decimal}. The F1 score came in at [0.89] {research:f1 ^^xsd:decimal} and AUC-ROC at [0.93] {research:auc ^^xsd:decimal}, both statistically significant at [p < 0.05] {research:significance} across a sample of [1,000] {research:sampleSize ^^xsd:integer} patients.
